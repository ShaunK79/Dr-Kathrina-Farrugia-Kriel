The Oxford Handbook of Ballet Pedagogy (forthcoming)- Oxford University Press

Edited by Dr Adesola Akinleye and Dr Kathrina Farrugia-Kriel, The Oxford Handbook of Ballet Pedagogy explores contemporary approaches to teaching ballet, emphasizing the embodied nature of dance and its role in shaping student expression and artistic development.

Comprised of 44 chapters, the book presents global perspectives from scholars, educators, and artists, that highlight how ballet education is evolving in response to cultural and social changes. integrates theory and practice, focusing on the whole dancer - body, identity, and well-being.

The book provides a comprehensive, inclusive framework for teaching ballet in the 21st century, aimed at educators and practitioners. It is due out in early 2026.
